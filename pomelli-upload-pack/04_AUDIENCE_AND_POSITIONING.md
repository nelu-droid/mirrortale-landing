# MirrorTale Audience And Positioning

Primary customers:
Parents and family gift buyers who want something more meaningful than a toy, voucher, or generic personalized item.

Secondary customers:
Grandparents, godparents, aunts, uncles, and friends looking for a heartfelt gift that feels personal and premium.

Child age range:
Best suited for families with young children, especially preschool and early primary ages.

Customer motivations:

- "I want a gift that feels personal."
- "I want my child to feel seen."
- "I want a bedtime book we can read together."
- "I want something more lasting than another toy."
- "I want a beautiful book that includes the child's interests."
- "I want a story that makes a routine or milestone feel less intimidating."

Buying objections to answer:

- Will the quality feel premium?
- Will the story actually feel personal?
- How fast do I receive it?
- Can I print it?
- Is there a physical version?
- Is family information handled respectfully?
- Will someone check the result before delivery?

Competitive frame:
MirrorTale should not look like a cheap novelty product. It should feel closer to a boutique children's publisher, a thoughtful family gift studio, and a magical keepsake maker.

Best positioning line:
A premium personalized storybook made from the details that make a child feel known.

Tone with parents:
Speak as a calm creative partner. Do not exaggerate parenting pain. Do not imply a child needs fixing. Avoid guilt, pressure, fear, or "problem child" framing.

Tone with gift buyers:
Emphasize delight, specificity, keepsake value, and the emotional moment of seeing a custom book made just for that child.
