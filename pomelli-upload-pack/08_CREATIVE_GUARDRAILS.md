# MirrorTale Creative Guardrails

Use these guardrails for marketing and generated visuals.

Safe creative focus:

- Finished books
- Hardcover mockups
- Cover art
- Open pages
- Bedtime tables
- Wrapped gifts
- Parent hands holding the book
- Cozy family spaces
- Magical illustrated story worlds

Avoid:

- Making a real child face the subject of generated marketing
- Before-and-after face comparisons
- Claims that the product fixes a child
- Medical, therapy, diagnosis, or behavior-cure claims
- Shaming children or parents
- Fear-based parenting hooks
- Overpromising results
- Using private family details as an ad gimmick

When showing people:

- Prefer hands, silhouettes, over-shoulder reading scenes, or illustrated storybook characters.
- Keep the finished book as the hero of the image.
- If a child appears, make the scene gentle, non-realistic, and storybook-like.

Claims to use carefully:

- "Digital files arrive in 24-48h"
- "Hardcover shipping can take up to 15 business days"
- "Manually checked before delivery"
- "Premium personalized storybook"
- "Optional hardcover keepsake"

Claims to avoid:

- "Guaranteed to solve bedtime"
- "Stops tantrums"
- "Therapeutic"
- "Scientifically proven"
- "Fix your child's routine"
- "Perfect likeness guaranteed"

Best default prompt instruction:
Create premium storybook marketing for MirrorTale. Focus on the finished personalized book as a gift-ready object. Use deep teal, forest green, warm gold, soft cream, and magical light. Keep the tone warm, parent-aware, premium, and trustworthy.
